How to learn from Coursera MOOCs offered in a language you don’t understand?

Housecat

The Solution

Well, with a little bit of extra effort (but of course!). But this extra effort need not be too complicated -

  1. Enroll in the course (Duh!)

  2. Download the lecture videos & the subtitles to your laptop/PC (really important that you do this)

  3. Translate the subtitles (which usually end with ‘.vtt’) using any website which offers to translate subtitles - I prefer Subtitle Translator which is free to use1

  4. Watch lecture videos with the subtitles on - If you can binge watch Netflix series in different languages, then you certainly can learn from Coursera MOOCs in different languages too!

Luckily, the courses I enrolled for were mathematical in which most the slides contained equations & diagrams - meaning less words - which mostly don’t need to be translated

FYI

The Original Problem

I wanted to pursue some courses related to transportation engineering & planning, which are taught in Spanish.

My initial solution was to translate the transcripts, which was stupid, given that it isn’t easy to follow the lecture video & the translated transcript simultaneously. This new problem prompted me to use this workaround of translating the subtitles file, instead.

The Courses

Coursera offers many courses which are not taught in English. The ones I ended taking up are on transportation planning (I’ve mentioned their names only in English for brevity’s sake) -

  • Transportation System Analysis - which is taught by, amongst others, Prof. Juan de Dios Ortuzar - whose textbook “Modelling Transport” has led to far too many over-caffeinated study sessions than I care to admit.

  • Introduction to Transport Demand Models - which, again, is taught by, amongst others, Prof. Juan de Dios Ortuzar

  • Traffic Engineering

  • How do we travel around the city? Allocation and balance in transport networks

If you are really interested in the subject & want a more approachable resource, then the co-author of the aforementioned book Luis Willumsen has written a new book on traffic forecasting (geared towards practitioners) - Better Traffic & Revenue Forecasting
